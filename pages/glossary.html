Glossary

We strive to not only work with seasoned professionals but allow those starting their adventure in property investment access to free educational content about various investment strategies and available tools.


This page is regularly updated with the latest terminology to keep you clued up on property investment.

BMV

Property Below Market Value. Investors who specialize in purchasing BMV properties often look for distressed properties, such as those in need of repairs, foreclosures, or properties being sold by motivated sellers due to financial difficulties, divorces, or other personal reasons. This typically occurs when a motivated seller needs to sell the property quickly or when the property has certain issues or circumstances that affect its marketability.

BRRR

Stands for Buy, Refurbish, Refinance, Rent, is a popular property investment strategy that focuses on generating cash flow and building a real estate portfolio over time. The BRRR strategy allows investors to acquire properties (often below market value - BMV), increase their value through renovations or improvements, rent them out to generate income, and then refinance to recoup their initial investment and repeat the process.It's important to note that the BRRRR strategy involves careful analysis, due diligence, and a thorough understanding of the local real estate market. It requires a strong financial foundation, access to financing, and the ability to accurately assess the costs involved in renovations and property management. Additionally, market conditions and property appreciation rates can impact the success of the strategy.

BTL

It is a property investment strategy where an individual or investor purchases a property with the intention of renting it out to tenants and generating rental income. The primary objective of a buy-to-let investment is to earn rental returns and potentially benefit from property price appreciation over time. In a Buy-To-Let scenario, the property owner (the landlord) often takes on the responsibility of maintaining the property, managing tenant relationships, and handling rental income and expenses. The rental income generated from tenants is used to cover costs such as mortgage payments, property maintenance, insurance, property management fees (if applicable), and other associated expenses.
